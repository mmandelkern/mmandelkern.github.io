undergraduate faqs

Detailed information about our major, minor, and honors program can be found here. Information about our course offerings can be found here.

If you have a question about transferring credit or counting a course from another department for philosophy credit, please send your question to Jack Mikuszewski, including as much information as possible in your email.

You should also write to Jack if you wish to declare the major or minor.

If you wish to take a course but have not met the prerequisites, you must email the course instructor to ask for an exception. I cannot help you with this.

If you want an exception to a major requirement, please send me a detailed explanation. Since the minor has so few requirements we are not flexible about changes to the minor.

I do not know of any internships or summer work opportunities related to the major.

If you want to take an independent study, please gather the information specified in the course description and then complete this form.

If you want general advice about what courses to take, my advice is to take logic and an introductory course as early as possible. Otherwise, take whatever courses interest you most in a given semester, consistent with completing the major requirements in time; keep an eye on the history requirements.

If you wish to dispute a grade, you must first meet with the instructor who gave you that grade. A subsequent dispute is appropriate only if you believe your grade violates a specific CAS policy. If you do wish to file an appeal, please send me and the department chair an explanation of the reason for the appeal, mentioning the specific policy that was violated, along with supporting documentation and a record of your past communication with the instructor.

If you have questions which were not answered by the above, please email me with as much detail as possible; let me know if you'd like to meet in person.
